Games

The Legend of Zelda

The Legend of Zelda (1986) The first game in the series introduced players to Link, a young hero tasked with rescuing Princess Zelda from the evil sorcerer Ganon. The game’s open-world design was revolutionary for its time, allowing players to explore Hyrule and tackle dungeons in any order they wished. Players collected pieces of the Triforce of Wisdom to defeat Ganon and save Zelda.

The Adventure of Link

Zelda II: The Adventure of Link (1987) A departure from the original’s top-down perspective, this sequel introduced side-scrolling action and RPG elements. Link embarks on a quest to break a curse on Princess Zelda. This game received mixed reviews due to its departure from the formula of the original game, but it still holds a unique place in the series' history.

A Link to the Past

The Legend of Zelda: A Link to the Past (1991) Returning to the top-down view, A Link to the Past introduced many elements that would become staples in the series, including the dual-world mechanic (the Light and Dark worlds) and the concept of magical items. Link’s mission is to rescue the seven maidens and defeat Ganon, further solidifying the series’ mythos.

Link's Awakening

The Legend of Zelda: Link's Awakening (1993) Originally released on the Game Boy, Link’s Awakening features a unique story set on the island of Koholint, where Link must awaken the Wind Fish to escape. It is one of the first games in the series to experiment with a more self-contained narrative and remains beloved for its charm and quirky characters.

Ocarina of Time

The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time (1998) Widely considered one of the greatest video games ever made, Ocarina of Time was the first 3D Zelda game and introduced the concept of time travel. Link, as both a child and an adult, must stop Ganondorf from obtaining the Triforce and conquering Hyrule. The game’s intricate puzzles, world-building, and revolutionary 3D gameplay set new standards for the gaming industry.

Majora's Mask

The Legend of Zelda: Majora’s Mask (2000) Released shortly after Ocarina of Time, Majora’s Mask features a darker tone and a unique time-based mechanic. Link must prevent the moon from colliding with the world by repeating a three-day cycle, making the game feel both urgent and introspective. It is often praised for its emotional depth and experimental gameplay.

Oracle of Seasons

The Legend of Zelda: Oracle of Seasons (2001) In Oracle of Seasons, Link arrives in Holodrum, where he witnesses Onox, the General of Darkness, kidnap Din, the Oracle of Seasons. Without her, the seasons in Holodrum spiral out of control. Link must wield the Rod of Seasons to shift between spring, summer, fall, and winter, using seasonal changes to navigate dungeons and the overworld. With the help of Impa, the Maku Tree, and Subrosians, Link collects the Essences of Nature to battle Onox and restore balance.

Oracle of Ages

The Legend of Zelda: Oracle of Ages (2001) In Oracle of Ages, Link is transported to the land of Labrynna, where he finds that the sorceress Veran has possessed Nayru, the Oracle of Ages. Using Nayru’s power, Veran manipulates time to bring chaos to the land. Link must use the Harp of Ages to travel between the past and present, solving puzzles and altering history to restore balance. With the help of allies like Ralph and the Maku Tree, Link gathers the Essences of Time to confront Veran and free Nayru.

The Wind Waker

The Legend of Zelda: The Wind Waker (2002) Featuring a cel-shaded art style, The Wind Waker divides fans due to its cartoonish visuals but is cherished for its deep exploration and naval combat mechanics. Set in a flooded world, Link embarks on a quest to rescue his sister and defeat the evil Ganon.

The Minish Cap

The Legend of Zelda: The Minish Cap (2004) Link partners with Ezlo, a talking cap, to shrink down to Minish size, collect the elements needed to reforge the broken Picori Blade (which then becomes the Four Sword), and stop Vaati from taking over Hyrule.

Twilight Princess

The Legend of Zelda: Twilight Princess (2006) Twilight Princess returns to a more realistic art style and features darker themes. Link transforms into a wolf in certain parts of the game, utilizing both human and wolf abilities to solve puzzles and defeat enemies. The game’s darker tone and expansive world received widespread acclaim.

Phantom Hourglass

The Legend of Zelda: Phantom Hourglass (2007) A sequel to The Wind Waker, Link and Tetra explore the Great Sea until Tetra is kidnapped by the Ghost Ship. Teaming up with a fairy named Ciela and a greedy sailor named Linebeck, Link sets off to awaken the sealed faires, battling the evil Bellum while solving puzzles with touchscreen controls.

Spirit Tracks

The Legend of Zelda: Spirit Tracks (2009) In Spirit Tracks, Link is a train engineer who teams up with the ghost of Princess Zelda after an evil force, Malladus, steals her body. The Spirit Tracks, magical train tracks that keep Malladus sealed, start disappearing, so Link and Zelda travel by train to restore them. Along the way, Zelda can possess Phantom Knights to help solve puzzles. They visit temples, defeat powerful enemies, and restore the tracks. In the final battle, Zelda regains her body, and together, they defeat Malladus, saving the kingdom.

Skyward Sword

The Legend of Zelda: Skyward Sword (2011) Utilizing motion controls for swordplay, Skyward Sword tells the origin story of Link and Zelda, exploring the creation of the Master Sword. The game received mixed feedback regarding its motion controls but was praised for its innovative gameplay and deep lore.

Breath of the Wild

The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild (2017) Breath of the Wild represents a significant departure from traditional Zelda gameplay. It features an open-world design with a focus on exploration, survival, and freedom of choice. Link’s quest to defeat Calamity Ganon is set in a vast, dynamic world with the most expansive and interactive environment in the series.

Tears of the Kingdom

The Legend of Zelda: Tears of the Kingdom (2023) A direct sequel to Breath of the Wild, Link once again finds himself saving Hyrule, but this time he uses new abilities to explore the sky and an underground area known as the Depths. Together with the sages, he works to defeat Ganondorf and save Zelda.
